[h1]NPC Passengers[/h1]
[i]Board NPCs into vehicles with full animation, emotional state tracking, and multi-vehicle framework support.[/i]

[hr][/hr]

[h2]Changelog[/h2]

[h3]v2.4 — March 2026[/h3]
[list]
[*] [b]Performance — Seat Discovery Cache:[/b] Vehicle seat layout is now cached per vehicle and only rebuilt when the vehicle's children change. Eliminates repeated seat traversal and sort on every boarding query.
[*] [b]Performance — Animation Timer Throttling:[/b] Per-passenger maintenance work (enemy tracking, relationship updates, head look, transform sync) is now gated by independent time intervals instead of running on every tick. Significantly reduces per-NPC CPU cost at higher passenger counts.
[*] [b]Performance — Client Entity Tracking:[/b] The body-sway hook no longer scans all entities every frame. Passengers are tracked in a dedicated table and refreshed once per second, removing the per-frame [i]ents.GetAll()[/i] call entirely.
[*] [b]QoL — Vehicle Filtering:[/b] Added server-side allow and deny lists for vehicle classes and models ([i]nai_npc_allow_classes[/i], [i]nai_npc_deny_classes[/i], [i]nai_npc_allow_models[/i], [i]nai_npc_deny_models[/i]) with CSV wildcard pattern support.
[*] [b]QoL — Boarding Retry Cooldown:[/b] NPCs that fail to board repeatedly now enter a cooldown period instead of looping indefinitely. Attempt limit and cooldown duration are configurable.
[*] [b]QoL — Squad-Only Auto-Join:[/b] Auto-Join can now be restricted to the player's active squad ([i]nai_npc_auto_join_squad_only[/i]).
[*] [b]QoL — Global Enable Toggle:[/b] Added [i]nai_npc_enabled[/i] convar to disable all addon functionality server-side without uninstalling.
[*] [b]Bug Fix:[/b] Fixed an early-return defect in vehicle seat collection that would return an uninitialized seat list when the vehicle cache was cold, causing boarding to silently fail on first attempt.
[*] [b]Code — Removed Nai Base API dependency:[/b] All internal identifiers previously prefixed with [i]Nai[/i] or [i]NaiPassengers[/i] have been renamed to [i]NPCPassengers[/i]. The addon no longer has any dependency on or coupling to the Nai's Base API. Saved ConVar names ([i]nai_npc_*[/i]) are unchanged to preserve existing user configurations.
[/list]

[h2]Core Features[/h2]

[h3]Animation & Physical Response[/h3]
[list]
[*] [b]Body Sway:[/b] NPCs physically lean with the vehicle through turns, acceleration, and braking. Intensity is adjustable.
[*] [b]Crash Flinch:[/b] A high-speed deceleration event triggers a flinch and bracing animation. Threshold is configurable.
[*] [b]Head Tracking:[/b] NPCs track the driver, nearby threats, and points of interest. Movement smoothing is tunable.
[*] [b]Eye Movement:[/b] Micro-glances independent of the head for natural-looking gaze behavior.
[*] [b]Blinking:[/b] Automatic eyelid animation with randomized interval timing.
[*] [b]Breathing:[/b] Subtle idle breathing cycle animation.
[*] [b]Talking Gestures:[/b] Random hand and body gesture animations play during idle conversation periods.
[/list]

[h3]Emotional State System[/h3]
[list]
[*] NPCs transition between [b]CALM[/b], [b]ALERT[/b], [b]SCARED[/b], [b]DROWSY[/b], and [b]DEAD[/b] states based on conditions in the environment.
[*] [b]Threat Awareness:[/b] Detects enemies within a configurable range and shifts NPCs to ALERT state. Head tracks the threat direction.
[*] [b]Fear Reactions:[/b] High speed or erratic driving transitions NPCs toward SCARED.
[*] [b]Drowsiness:[/b] After an extended period of calm travel, NPCs gradually become drowsy with appropriate head nod behavior.
[*] [b]Crash Damage:[/b] High-speed impacts deal health damage to passengers. Severity scales with deceleration force.
[/list]

[h3]NPC Speech & Audio[/h3]
[list]
[*] [b]Boarding Lines:[/b] NPCs speak context-aware lines when entering or exiting the vehicle.
[*] [b]Idle Chatter:[/b] Occasional conversation during travel with configurable frequency and chance.
[*] [b]Passenger Interaction:[/b] Multiple passengers will look at and talk to one another.
[*] [b]Crash Reactions:[/b] Pain sounds and verbal responses on impact events.
[*] [b]Ambient Sounds:[/b] Low-frequency ambient sounds such as coughs, sighs, and hums with configurable interval.
[*] [b]Pitch Variation:[/b] Random pitch offset per NPC for natural vocal differentiation.
[/list]

[h3]Auto-Join System[/h3]
[list]
[*] Nearby friendly NPCs automatically board available seats when the player enters a vehicle.
[*] Configurable search range and maximum count per vehicle.
[*] Optional [b]squad-only mode[/b]: only NPCs in the player's active squad will board.
[*] Can be toggled at any time via keybind or settings panel.
[/list]

[h3]Multi-Passenger & Boarding Logic[/h3]
[list]
[*] Supports up to 8 passengers per vehicle (configurable hard cap).
[*] Manual queuing via right-click context menu allows targeting specific NPCs before boarding.
[*] Seat discovery is framework-aware and never assigns the driver seat or an already-occupied seat.
[*] Dead passengers do not count toward seat occupancy.
[*] Retry system with configurable attempt limit and cooldown on repeated boarding failures.
[/list]

[h3]Dead Body Management[/h3]
[list]
[*] Dead passengers remain in the vehicle, visually indicated with a red glow and skull marker on the HUD.
[*] Hold [b]R[/b] while near a dead body to remove it from the vehicle.
[/list]

[h3]Vehicle Filtering[/h3]
[list]
[*] Server-side allow and deny lists for vehicle [b]classes[/b] and [b]models[/b] using comma-separated patterns with wildcard ([b]*[/b]) support.
[*] Deny lists are evaluated before allow lists. An empty allow list permits all non-denied vehicles.
[/list]

[h3]NPC Driver & Gunner (LVS / Simfphys / Glide)[/h3]
[list]
[*] Assign an NPC as the [b]driver[/b] of a supported vehicle. The NPC will navigate toward a target using pathfinding.
[*] Air vehicle support for LVS helicopters and planes.
[*] Assign an NPC to an [b]LVS turret seat[/b] for autonomous fire support.
[/list]

[h2]Passenger Status HUD[/h2]
[list]
[*] Displays each passenger's current state (CALM / ALERT / SCARED / DROWSY / DEAD) in real time.

[h2]Quick Start[/h2]

[b]Auto-Join (recommended):[/b]
Press [b]F7[/b] → navigate to the [b]Auto-Join[/b] tab → enable Auto-Join → enter a vehicle. Nearby friendly NPCs will board automatically.

[b]Manual:[/b]
Hold [b]C[/b] → right-click an NPC → [b]Make Passenger[/b] → enter a vehicle. The NPC will walk to and board the nearest available seat.

[b]Remove a passenger:[/b]
Right-click the passenger while in the context menu, or select [b]Detach Passenger[/b].

[b]Remove a dead body:[/b]
Approach the vehicle and hold [b]R[/b].

[h2]Common Issues[/h2]

[b]NPC is floating above the seat:[/b] Use the Position Offset controls in the settings panel (F7) to correct the seating alignment for that vehicle model.
[b]NPC keeps dying in crashes:[/b] Increase the crash damage threshold in the settings, or reduce crash damage scaling.
[b]Dead body cannot be removed:[/b] Move closer to the vehicle and hold R for the full duration.
[b]Auto-Join is not triggering:[/b] Verify the search range and maximum passenger count in the Auto-Join settings tab. Ensure the NPCs are in a friendly relation to the player.
[b]Passengers not boarding a specific vehicle:[/b] Check whether the vehicle class or model matches an entry in the deny filter convars ([i]nai_npc_deny_classes[/i], [i]nai_npc_deny_models[/i]).
